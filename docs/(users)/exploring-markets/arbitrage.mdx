## One idea, two strategies

Arbitrage buys the **same number of shares in two opposite outcomes**. The labels can be
Yes/No, Up/Down, or two competing teams—the important part is that one outcome pays
**$1.00** at resolution and the other pays **$0.00**.

<Callout type="info" title="The rule">
  **Yes cost + No cost + estimated fees must be below $1.00 per matched pair.**
</Callout>

## The two-leg calculation

The formula is:

**Matched shares × ($1.00 − combined executable price − fees per matched pair) = estimated profit**

For the example: **100 × ($1.00 − $0.95 − $0.01) = $4.00**. The displayed
**$1.00 estimated fees** is the total for all 100 matched pairs.

<Callout type="warning" title="The numbers above are illustrative">
  Always use the live quote in the order panel. It calculates the available prices across
  the order books, fees, balances, minimum order sizes, and the exact quantity that both
  legs can match.
</Callout>

## Why the payout is the same either way

<Cards>
  <Card icon={<ShieldCheckIcon />} title="If Outcome A wins">
    100 Outcome A shares pay **$100**. Outcome B pays **$0**.
  </Card>

  <Card icon={<ShieldCheckIcon />} title="If Outcome B wins">
    100 Outcome B shares pay **$100**. Outcome A pays **$0**.
  </Card>

  <Card icon={<CircleDollarSignIcon />} title="Either resolution">
    The matched position pays **$100** in total. In the example, its estimated cost was
    **$96**.
  </Card>
</Cards>

This payout logic assumes both legs fill in equal quantities and remain held through
resolution.

## How to place an arbitrage trade

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Open Arbitrage

    Select **Arbitrage** in the order panel, then choose **Outcome** or **Polymarket**.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Review both legs

    Confirm the outcome and executable price shown for each leg. Open the profit tooltip
    to review costs, estimated fees, payout, and return.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Choose the matched amount

    Enter an amount or use Min, Mid, or Max. Max is limited by profitable liquidity and
    the balance required by both legs.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Sign both orders

    Each leg requires an order signature. Outcome orders are submitted together to the
    <SiteName /> CLOB; Polymarket arbitrage submits one order to each platform.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Verify and hold

    Check the completion message and your positions. After both legs fill, hold the equal
    opposite shares through resolution and claim the winning position.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Two legs do not mean guaranteed atomic execution">
  Each order uses fill-or-kill execution, but the pair is not a single atomic blockchain
  transaction. If one leg fills and the other fails, you may temporarily hold directional
  exposure. Check the reported status and your positions before trying again.
</Callout>

## Common questions

<Accordions type="single" collapsible>
  <Accordion title="Do the outcomes need to be called Yes and No?" value="outcome-names">
    No. Outcome arbitrage works with any two complementary outcomes, including Up/Down
    and team names. The panel matches the two underlying outcome tokens.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need a Polymarket wallet?" value="polymarket-wallet">
    Only for the Polymarket strategy. Outcome arbitrage uses your <SiteName /> trading
    wallet and balance for both legs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why is Max lower than my balance?" value="maximum-amount">
    Max is limited by your available balance, profitable liquidity shared by both legs,
    and the exact fill-or-kill order amounts after exchange quantization.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I sell before resolution?" value="exit-early">
    Yes, but the displayed arbitrage profit assumes both matched legs are held through
    resolution. Selling early creates new trades at current prices and changes the result.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
